## Design Context

### Users
People with scattered attention who need a frictionless place to dump thoughts and tasks daily. They open the app at the start of their day, jot things down, drag them where they feel right, and move on. They don't want to manage — they want to do.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant, Calm, Effortless**

- Voice: Quiet confidence. Never loud, never busy.
- Tone: Like a well-made notebook — says nothing, invites everything.
- Emotional goal: The user should feel a sense of relief and clarity when opening the app. No anxiety about unfinished items. No guilt. Just a clean page.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Refined minimalism with warmth. Think Things 3 — every pixel is intentional, nothing is decorative for decoration's sake.
- **Reference**: Things 3 — the spacing, the restraint, the subtle depth, the way it feels premium without being flashy.
- **Anti-references**: Jira/Asana (overwhelming complexity, dense UI, management overhead) and Todoist (rigid list structure, too much forced organization). BetterDay should never feel like a "productivity tool" — it should feel like a piece of paper.
- **Theme**: Light and dark mode. Grayscale foundation with subtle warm accents (amber/sand tones) for interactive elements and gentle emphasis.
- **Typography**: Geist Sans — clean, modern, excellent readability. Use weight and size for hierarchy, not color or decoration.
- **Spacing**: Generous. Let content breathe. White space is a feature, not waste.
- **Motion**: Subtle and purposeful. Cards should feel physical — smooth drags, gentle snaps. No bouncy animations or flashy transitions.

### Design Principles

1. **Invisible interface** — The best UI is one you don't notice. Chrome should recede; content should dominate. If you can remove an element without losing function, remove it.
2. **Warmth through restraint** — Elegance comes from what you leave out. Use warm accents sparingly — a hint of amber on an active state, a sand tone on a hover. Never saturated, never competing with content.
3. **Physical metaphor** — Cards should feel like real objects on a desk. They have weight (subtle shadow), they can be moved (smooth drag), they occupy space (no overlapping). The whiteboard is a surface, not a list.
4. **Respect for time** — Each day is finite and complete. The UI should reinforce this — a bounded canvas, not an infinite scroll. Yesterday is turned, tomorrow is blank.
5. **Premium in the details** — The difference between good and great is in the micro-interactions: how a card appears when created, how it settles after being dragged, how a checkbox feels when ticked. These moments define the brand.
